# Enroll in Supplemental Feeding Programs — Municipality of Allen

The Municipal Nutrition Action Office (MNAO) implements supplemental and community feeding programs for malnourished children under five, school children, pregnant women, and lactating mothers. Beneficiaries are identified based on their current nutritional status and referred by the Barangay Nutrition Scholar (BNS), school nurse, or Rural Health Unit (RHU).

## How to Enroll

### Step 1: Present the list of children or beneficiaries to the MNAO

- The Barangay Nutrition Scholar (BNS), school nurse, or RHU presents the list of children with their nutritional status to the MNAO.
- For lactating and pregnant women, present the list with their current weight and height measurements.
- MNAO staff receives the list (approximately 5 minutes) and forwards it to the MNAO Designate for review (approximately 10 minutes).

### Step 2: Nutritional status assessment

- The RND assesses the nutritional status of each child on the list (approximately 5 minutes per client).
- Children are categorized based on their condition:
  - **Underweight and severely underweight**
  - **Stunted and severely stunted**
  - **Wasted and severely wasted**
- Severe cases are reported to the RHU for further management (approximately 1 hour for coordination).

### Step 3: Receive the nutrition pack

- Eligible beneficiaries receive their nutrition pack (feeding supplement) from MNAO staff (approximately 30 minutes).

## Requirements

### Documents Needed

- [ ] Referral slip _(if available)_ — _OPD/OTC/ITC_
- [ ] Child Record with nutritional status (per barangay) — _Barangay Nutrition Scholar (BNS)_
- [ ] School children list — _School Nurse_
- [ ] List of pregnant and lactating women with current weight and height — _BNS or RHU_

### Eligibility

- Children aged 0–59 months who are underweight, stunted, or wasted
- School children with identified nutritional deficiencies
- Pregnant and lactating women with nutritional needs
