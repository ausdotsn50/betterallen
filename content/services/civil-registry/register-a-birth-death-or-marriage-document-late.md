# Register a Birth, Death, or Marriage Document Late — Municipality of Allen

If a birth, death, or marriage was not registered within the required period, you can still file a late (delayed) registration at the Municipal Civil Registry Office. Late registration is accepted beyond the 30-day reglementary period for births and deaths, and beyond 15 days for ordinary marriages.

---

| Information                   | Details                                                                                     |

| **Classification**            | Simple Transaction                                                                          |
| **Type of Transaction**       | Government to Citizen                                                                       |
| **Who May Avail**             | Attendant at birth or death, mother, father, guardian, or nearest kin of the document owner |
| **Estimated Processing Time** | 20 minutes (documents subject to 10 days of posting)                                        |
| **Fees**                      | ₱150.00                                                                                     |

---

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Proceed to the Municipal Civil Registry Office and present your documents

- Submit all required documents to the Civil Registry staff.
- Staff will receive, examine, and verify the requirements (approximately 5 minutes).

### Step 2: Pay at the Municipal Treasurer's Office

- Proceed to the MTO and pay the registration fee of ₱150.00.
- An Official Receipt will be issued.

### Step 3: Return to the Civil Registry and present your Official Receipt

- Return to the Municipal Civil Registry Office and present your Official Receipt.
- The Municipal Civil Registrar evaluates the veracity of the submitted documents through an interview and other means of verification (approximately 5 minutes).

### Step 4: Receive your Notice of Posting

- Staff issues a Notice of Posting, which will be posted for **10 consecutive days** (approximately 2 minutes).

### Step 5: Return after 10 days to claim your document

- Return to the Civil Registry Office after the 10-day posting period.
- The Municipal Civil Registrar reviews and registers the document (approximately 3 minutes).
- Claim your registered civil registry document.

## Requirements

### For Late Registration of **Birth**

- See the checklist for delayed registration of birth available at the Civil Registry Office.

### For Late Registration of **Death**

- [ ] 4 copies of Certificate of Death (COD)
- [ ] Certificate of No Death (CENODEATH) — _Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA)_
- [ ] Affidavit for delayed registration (at the back of the COD):
  - Executed by the hospital/clinic administrator if the person died in a hospital, clinic, or similar institution
  - Executed by the attendant at death if the person died elsewhere
- [ ] Approval for registration by the Health Officer in the COD
- [ ] Authenticated copy of the certification of burial, cremation, or from the cemetery or funeral parlor
- [ ] Affidavit of Two (2) Disinterested Persons

### For Late Registration of **Marriage**

- [ ] 4 copies of Certificate of Marriage (COM)
- [ ] Certificate of No Marriage (CENOMAR) — _Philippine Statistics Authority (PSA)_
- [ ] Affidavit of Two (2) Disinterested Persons
