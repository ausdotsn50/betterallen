# Access Intervention and Diversion Programs for Children in Conflict with the Law — Municipality of Allen

The MSWDO provides psycho-social intervention and diversion programs for children in conflict with the law (CICL) in accordance with Republic Act No. 9344 (Juvenile Justice and Welfare Act). The program focuses on rehabilitation, accountability, and reintegration rather than punitive measures.

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Report or referral to MSWDO

- The child is referred to the MSWDO by the Barangay, PNP, or concerned party.
- Staff records the case in the client logbook.
- An intake interview and verification of submitted reports is conducted (approximately 1 hour or more).

### Step 2: Assessment of discernment and home visitation

- The Social Worker assesses the child's discernment (approximately 1 hour or more).
- A home visitation is conducted to gather additional information (may take 1 day or more).
- A Social Case Study Report with a treatment plan is prepared (approximately 3–5 hours).

### Step 3: Preparation of Intervention or Diversion Plan

- The Social Worker prepares the Intervention Plan or Diversion Contract (approximately 1–2 hours).
- This is done in coordination with the Barangay Council for the Protection of Children (BCPC), PNP, and other concerned agencies.

### Step 4: Implementation and monitoring of the program

- The Intervention or Diversion Program is implemented and monitored (within 45 days, with ongoing coordination).
- The case is evaluated for termination once the program objectives are achieved (1 day or more).
- A Terminal Report is prepared upon case closure (approximately 1–2 hours).

## Requirements

### Documents Needed

- [ ] Barangay or Police Report — _Barangay Hall or PNP_

### Eligibility

- Children below 18 years old who are in conflict with the law and referred to the MSWDO.
