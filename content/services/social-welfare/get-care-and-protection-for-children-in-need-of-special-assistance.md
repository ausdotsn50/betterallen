# Get Care and Protection for Children in Need of Special Assistance — Municipality of Allen

The MSWDO provides assistance, case management, and access to intervention services for children in need of special protection (CNSP) — including street children, and children who are abandoned, physically abused, sexually abused, or emotionally abused.

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Seek assistance or be referred to the MSWDO

- The child or a concerned party approaches the MSWDO directly, or the case is referred by the Barangay or PNP.
- Staff records the case in the client logbook.
- An intake interview and verification of submitted reports is conducted (approximately 1 hour or more).

### Step 2: Home visitation and assessment

- The Social Worker conducts a home visitation to gather more information about the child's situation (may take 1 day or more).
- An assessment and treatment plan is prepared through a Social Case Study Report (approximately 3–5 hours).

### Step 3: Implementation of treatment plan and access to services

- The treatment plan is implemented and monitored in coordination with DOH/MHO, DepEd, DSWD, and other relevant agencies (6 months to 1 year).
- The case is evaluated for termination once goals are achieved (1 day or more).
- A Terminal Report is prepared upon case closure (approximately 1–2 hours).

## Requirements

### Documents Needed

- [ ] Barangay or Police Report — _Barangay Hall or PNP_
- [ ] Referral letter _(if referred by another agency or concerned party)_

### Eligibility

- Children who are abandoned, abused (physically, sexually, or emotionally), neglected, or living on the streets within or referred to Allen.
