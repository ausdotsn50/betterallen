# Process Legal Instruments Through the Civil Registrar — Municipality of Allen

The Municipal Civil Registry Office registers legal instruments that affect a person's civil status — such as Acknowledgment of Paternity (AAP), the Affidavit to Use the Surname of the Father (AUSF), and the Private Handwritten Instrument (PHI). These are administrative acts executed without a court order, under Republic Act No. 9255.

---

## What You Need to Know

| Information                   | Details                                                                  |
| ----------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Office**                    | Municipal Civil Registry Office                                          |
| **Classification**            | Simple Transaction                                                       |
| **Type of Transaction**       | Government to Citizen                                                    |
| **Who May Avail**             | Father, mother, the person themselves (if of legal age), or the guardian |
| **Estimated Processing Time** | Approximately 30 minutes                                                 |
| **Fees**                      | Acknowledgment of Paternity (AAP): ₱300.00; Legitimation: ₱150.00        |

---

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Proceed to the Civil Registry Office and present your complete requirements

- Submit all required documents to the Municipal Civil Registrar.
- Staff receives and evaluates the required documents (approximately 5 minutes).

### Step 2: Pay at the Municipal Treasurer's Office

- Proceed to the MTO and pay the applicable fee.
- An Official Receipt will be issued (approximately 5 minutes).

### Step 3: Return to the Civil Registry for registration and release

- Return and present your Official Receipt.
- The Civil Registrar registers the legal instrument and assigns a registry number in the Book of Registry of Legal Instruments (approximately 20 minutes).
- Copies of the registered legal instrument are distributed, including the copy for the document owner.

---

## Requirements

Requirements vary depending on the type of legal instrument. As a general rule, all legal instruments must be registered within **30 days** from the date of execution, except for AAP, PHI, and AUSF which must be registered within **20 days** under the Revised IRR of RA 9255.

Visit the Civil Registry Office and present your documents — staff will advise on any additional requirements.

---

## Types of Legal Instruments Covered

- **Acknowledgment of Paternity (AAP)** — filed by the father, mother, the person (if of legal age), or guardian
- **Affidavit to Use the Surname of the Father (AUSF)** — filed by the father, mother, the person (if of legal age), or guardian
- **Private Handwritten Instrument (PHI)** — must be personally filed by the father at the Civil Registry
