# Request Simulation Exercises and Drills — Municipality of Allen

The Municipal Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office (MDRRMO) facilitates disaster simulation exercises and drills for schools, barangays, and other organizations. These drills help communities practice emergency response procedures and improve preparedness.

---

| Information                   | Details                                                                       |
| ----------------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Office**                    | Municipal Disaster Risk Reduction and Management Office (MDRRMO)              |

| **Who May Avail**             | All agencies and organizations (schools, barangays, etc.)                     |
| **Estimated Processing Time** | 1 day for approval; drill conducted on scheduled date (approximately 2 hours) |

---

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Visit the MDRRMO and sign the client logbook

- Go to the MDRRMO and sign in at the information desk logbook (approximately 1 minute).

### Step 2: Submit your request letter

- Submit a request letter addressed to the Municipal Mayor, coursed through the Local Disaster Risk Reduction Management Officer (LDRRMO).
- Staff will check schedule availability and forward the letter to the Mayor's Office for LCE approval (approximately 10 minutes for receipt; 1 day for approval).
- Approval is subject to favorable weather conditions and availability of MDRRMO personnel.

### Step 3: Follow up after 1 day

- Return to the MDRRMO or call to follow up on the status of your request (approximately 4 minutes).
- If approved, the MDRRMO will set a schedule for the exercise.
- MDRRMO personnel will prepare materials for the drill (approximately 3 hours).

### Step 4: Participate in the drill on the scheduled date

- Wait for MDRRMO personnel on your agreed date and venue.
- MDRRMO facilitates the conduct of the simulation exercise or drill (approximately 2 hours).
- A debrief of participants' performance is conducted after the exercise.
