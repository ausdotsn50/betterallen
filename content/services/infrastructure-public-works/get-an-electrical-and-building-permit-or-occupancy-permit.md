# Get an Electrical and Building Permit or Occupancy Permit — Municipality of Allen

The Municipal Engineering Office issues Building Permits and Occupancy Permits for construction and structural projects in Allen. All construction must secure a Building Permit before work begins, in compliance with the National Building Code (PD 1096).

---

## What You Need to Know

| Information                   | Details                                                                                 |
| ----------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Office**                    | Municipal Engineering Office (MEO)                                                      |
| **Classification**            | Simple Transaction                                                                      |
| **Type of Transaction**       | Government to Citizen                                                                   |
| **Who May Avail**             | All applicants for a Building Permit or Occupancy Permit                                |
| **Estimated Processing Time** | 58 minutes (with complete requirements)                                                 |
| **Fees**                      | Based on PD 1096 (computed from structure and Bill of Materials); plus Notarization Fee |

---

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Request the requirements checklist

- Visit the Municipal Engineering Office and ask for the checklist of requirements for a Building Permit or Occupancy Permit.
- Staff will print and provide the checklist (approximately 5 minutes).

### Step 2: Submit your requirements

- Submit all required documents to the MEO.
- Staff will check that all requirements are complete and immediately inform you of any missing or deficient documents (approximately 5 minutes).

### Step 3: Fill out the application form

- Accomplish the Building Permit application form with assistance from MEO staff (approximately 15 minutes).

### Step 4: Request for computation of fees

- Request the computation of your Building Permit fee from MEO staff.
- Fees are based on PD 1096 and computed from the structure and Bill of Materials (approximately 30 minutes).

### Step 5: Pay fees at the Municipal Treasurer's Office (MTO)

- Proceed to the MTO to pay the computed Building Permit fee.
- The MTO will issue an Official Receipt.

### Step 6: Wait for approval by the Building Official

- The Building Official reviews and signs the approved permit (approximately 3 minutes).

### Step 7: Notarize the application form

- Have your application form notarized at a Public Notary Office.
- Notarization fees apply.
- The concerned office will sign and seal the documents.

## Requirements

### Documents Needed

- [ ] Barangay Certificate — _Barangay Hall_
- [ ] Certified True Copy (CTC) of Tax Declaration — _Municipal Treasurer's Office_
- [ ] Current Real Property Tax Receipt — _Municipal Treasurer's Office_
- [ ] Duly notarized copy of any of the following (as applicable): Contract of Lease, Deed of Absolute Sale, or Contract of Sale
- [ ] Five (5) sets of Plans and Specifications, prepared, signed, and sealed by:
  - Duly Licensed Architect or Civil Engineer
  - Duly Licensed Electrical Engineer
  - Duly Licensed Sanitary Engineer or Master Plumber
  - Duly Licensed Professional Mechanical Engineer _(if applicable)_
- [ ] Architectural documents including:
  - Floor Plan
  - Elevation
  - Section
  - Foundation Plan
  - Floor Framing Plan
  - Roof Framing Plan
  - Truss Detail Plan
  - Bill of Materials _(signed and sealed)_
- [ ] Approved Locational Clearance — _Zoning Office / MPDC_
