# Get Nutrition Assessment and Counseling — Municipality of Allen

The Municipal Nutrition Action Office (MNAO) provides individual nutritional assessments and counseling for malnourished children, pregnant women, and lactating mothers. Clients are assessed for severity of malnutrition and given appropriate interventions, supplements, and a monitoring schedule.

## How to Avail

### Step 1: Register on the Customer Assistance Sheet

- Sign in at the MNAO and register on the Customer Assistance Sheet.
- Staff checks any referral slip or requirements brought by the client (approximately 15 minutes).

### Step 2: Nutritional status assessment

- The Registered Nutritionist-Dietitian (RND) assesses the client's nutritional status and determines the severity of malnutrition (approximately 20 minutes).

### Step 3: Nutrition counseling and intervention

- The RND provides counseling to the parent, caregiver, or the pregnant or lactating woman (approximately 1 hour per group or individual).
- Appropriate nutrition interventions are provided based on the assessment:
  - **Underweight and stunted children** — micronutrient supplements and age-appropriate food supply (if available)
  - **Moderate Acute Malnutrition (MAM)** — micronutrient supplements and/or Ready-To-Use Supplementary Food (RUSF), if available
  - **Severe Acute Malnutrition (SAM) with complications** — referred to a hospital for in-patient care
  - **Pregnant and lactating women** — Ready-To-Use Supplementary Food for mothers and/or micronutrient supplements (if available)

### Step 4: Receive growth recording sheet and monitoring schedule

- The RND provides a growth recording sheet and sets a monitoring schedule for follow-up (approximately 10 minutes).
- The MNAO coordinates with the Barangay Nutrition Scholar (BNS) for follow-up and monitoring visits (approximately 20 minutes).

## Requirements

### Documents Needed

- [ ] Referral slip _(if available)_ — _OPD/OTC/ITC_

### Eligibility

- Malnourished children aged 0–59 months
- School-age children with nutritional concerns
- Pregnant and lactating women
