# Register a Court Decree — Municipality of Allen

The Municipal Civil Registry Office registers court decrees and decisions that affect a person's civil status — such as annulment, legal separation, adoption, divorce, and naturalization. Registration is required to update official civil registry records after a court ruling.

---

## How to Apply

### Step 1: Proceed to the Civil Registry Office and present your complete requirements

- Submit all required documents to the Municipal Civil Registrar.
- Staff receives, evaluates, and verifies the requirements (approximately 5 minutes).

### Step 2: Pay at the Municipal Treasurer's Office

- Proceed to the MTO and pay the applicable fee based on the type of court decree.
- An Official Receipt will be issued (approximately 5 minutes).

### Step 3: Return to the Civil Registry for registration and release

- Return and present your Official Receipt.
- The Civil Registrar prepares, registers, and assigns a registry number in the Book of Registry of Court Decrees (approximately 20 minutes).
- A Certificate of Registration and Certificate of Authenticity of the Court Decision are issued.

---

## Requirements

No standard checklist is pre-specified — bring your court-authenticated copy of the decision or order. The Civil Registrar will verify the authenticity of the document if filed by a person other than the Clerk of Court.
